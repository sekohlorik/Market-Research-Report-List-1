<p><p>The cluster munitions market comprises various types, primarily focusing on anti-tank and anti-personnel munitions. Anti-tank cluster munitions are designed to target armored vehicles and fortifications, using multiple submunitions to increase impact and coverage. In contrast, anti-personnel cluster munitions are intended to incapacitate or eliminate infantry, deploying smaller explosives over a wide area to maximize their effectiveness against troops. Both types raise significant ethical concerns due to their indiscriminate nature and potential for unexploded ordnance, affecting civilian populations long after conflicts end.</p></p>
