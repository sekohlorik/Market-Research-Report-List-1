<p><ul><li>Statins</li><li>Cholesterol Absorption Inhibitors</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Dyslipidemia drugs are categorized primarily into three types: Statins, Cholesterol Absorption Inhibitors, and Others. Statins are the most widely prescribed medications that lower cholesterol by inhibiting HMG-CoA reductase, effectively reducing LDL levels. Cholesterol Absorption Inhibitors, like ezetimibe, work by blocking the absorption of cholesterol in the intestine. The "Others" category includes various agents such as fibrates and PCSK9 inhibitors, which target triglycerides and further lower LDL cholesterol, offering different mechanisms for managing lipid levels.</p></p>
