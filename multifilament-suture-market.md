<p><p>The multifilament suture market is categorized into absorbable and non-absorbable sutures. Absorbable sutures are designed to dissolve over time in the body, making them ideal for internal stitching where removal is impractical. They are commonly made from materials like polyglycolic acid. Non-absorbable sutures, on the other hand, remain intact in the body, offering long-term support for wounds that require lasting strength, typically made from materials like silk or nylon. Both types cater to various surgical needs and preferences.</p></p>
