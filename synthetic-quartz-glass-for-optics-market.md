<p><p>Synthetic quartz glass for optics is produced using various methods, primarily the chemical vapor deposition (CVD) process and plasma deposition techniques. The CVD process involves the chemical reaction of gaseous precursors to form a thin layer of quartz on a substrate, enabling high-purity and uniform material. In contrast, plasma deposition utilizes ionized gas to create quartz layers, offering rapid deposition rates and versatile production. Both methods are essential for manufacturing high-performance optical components used in lasers, lenses, and fiber optics applications.</p></p>
