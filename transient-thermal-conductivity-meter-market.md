<p><p>The Transient Thermal Conductivity Meter market features two primary types: the Heat-line Method and the Heat-source Method. The Heat-line Method involves inserting a heated line into the material, measuring temperature changes over time to determine thermal conductivity. In contrast, the Heat-source Method uses a point source of heat applied to the sample's surface, tracking temperature fluctuations to analyze the material's thermal properties. Both methods are essential for assessing materials in various applications, including construction and manufacturing.</p></p>
